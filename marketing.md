> Last updated: May 11, 2026 | Stage: Validation (zero-budget organic only)

InstallNest is the only UK platform that connects EV buyers with rigorously vetted, OZEV-approved installers — completely free, with no obligation and no phone calls.

## Consumer Messaging

### Primary angle: "Know your total EV cost before you buy"

Target: Pre-purchase EV buyers who are evaluating total cost of ownership. They're Googling "how much does EV charger installation cost?" before committing to a car purchase.

**Hook:** EV charger installation costs vary wildly — from £600 to £1,500+. The only way to know your actual number is to get real quotes from real local installers. InstallNest does that for free, in hours.

**Supporting messages:**
- You may be eligible for up to £500 off through the government's EV Chargepoint Grant — most people don't know they qualify
- No pushy salespeople. Quotes land in your inbox, you decide if and when to respond
- Every installer on InstallNest is OZEV-approved and NICEIC-certified — not just anyone who paid to list

**CTA:** "Get free quotes" / "Check your installation cost" / "See if you qualify for the £500 grant"

---

### Secondary angle: "The trusted way to find a verified local installer"

Target: Existing EV owners who need a charger installed now and are wary of online directories.

**Hook:** Finding a trustworthy EV charger installer shouldn't require 10 phone calls and a leap of faith. InstallNest only lists installers who are OZEV-approved, NICEIC-certified, and have verified customer reviews.

---

### Tone of voice

| Attribute | What it sounds like |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Confident, not arrogant** | "Every installer is vetted" not "We're the best" |
| **Practical, not salesy** | Facts and process, not hype |
| **Warm, not corporate** | First-person language, plain English |
| **Empowering, not patronising** | "You decide" / "You're in control" |

**Avoid:** jargon, passive voice, vague superlatives ("best", "leading", "top"), anything that sounds like a Checkatrade clone.

## Installer Messaging

**Primary hook:** Stop paying for junk leads. Get pre-qualified, geographically targeted EV charger jobs from customers who are ready to act.

**Supporting messages:**
- Only receive leads in your exact service area — no wasted quotes
- Customers have already expressed intent — not cold traffic
- Build a verified review profile on an EV-specific platform (unlike generic Checkatrade)
- Pay only for the leads you receive — no upfront subscription during launch phase
- We handle the marketing; you focus on the installs

**CTA:** "Join as an installer" / "Start receiving leads in your area"

## Channel Strategy

### Current channels (validation phase)

#### SpeakEV Forum — **WORKING**
The strongest organic channel so far. EV enthusiasts actively discuss charger costs, installers, and grants.

**What to do:**
- Keep the thread alive with helpful, non-promotional replies
- Answer specific questions in the thread with depth — become the "expert who built a comparison platform"
- Add value first, drop the link only when directly relevant
- Start new threads in adjacent categories: "EV Chargepoint Grant eligibility guide", "How to assess an EV installer quote"
- Identify other active EV forums (Pistonheads EV section, ZapMap community, EVbite)

**What not to do:** Promote directly. SpeakEV will tolerate a builder sharing a project; they'll ban spam.

---

#### Facebook EV Groups — **PARTIALLY WORKING (moderation issues)**
High-intent audience exists in groups like "UK EV Owners", "Electric Vehicle Owners UK", "EV Advice UK". But promotional posts get removed.

**Workarounds:**
- Post as a genuine community member, not a brand — personal account, not the InstallNest page
- Answer questions about charger costs or installers, then mention the platform as a resource naturally in context
- Use the InstallNest page to share genuinely useful content (grant explainers, "what to look for in an EV installer" guides) — group members can share on your behalf
- Identify group admins and send a direct, honest message: "I've built a free tool for the community — would this be appropriate to share?"

---

#### Reddit — **BLOCKED (requires workarounds)**
Subreddits like r/electricvehicles, r/UKPersonalFinance, r/CarAdvice are highly relevant but auto-moderate new accounts with links.

**Workarounds:**
- Build karma in relevant subreddits before posting (30+ days of genuine participation)
- Post as a text-only question/discussion, mention the platform only in comments when asked
- r/UKPersonalFinance: Frame as a "I'm building a free comparison tool for EV charger installs, would this be useful?" — this community rewards genuine questions
- Consider a Product Hunt launch when MVP is ready — Reddit communities often pick these up organically

---

#### Installer outreach — **NOT STARTED**
Direct outreach to OZEV-approved installers is essential to prove two-sided interest.

**Where to find them:**
- OZEV's public register of approved installers (government website)
- NICEIC's installer directory
- Local Google searches for "EV charger installer [city]"

**Outreach approach:**
- Email or contact form: short, honest, founder-direct message
- Pitch: "I'm building a free lead-gen platform specifically for OZEV-approved installers. We're in early validation — I'd love to know if this is something you'd use."
- Goal for validation: 200–300 waitlist signups

## Content Strategy

### Now (validation phase)
All content should be useful-first, brand-second. The goal is to build trust and drive organic shares with zero budget.

**Content to create:**
1. **"How much does EV charger installation cost in the UK?" (2026 guide)** — SEO-ready, shareable in forums. Honest answer: £600–£1,500 depending on [factors]. CTA: get a free local quote via InstallNest.
2. **"Am I eligible for the EV Chargepoint Grant? Full guide"** — High-intent, grant-seekers are hot leads.
3. **"What to look for in an EV charger installer (and questions to ask)"** — Useful for pre-purchase buyers.
4. **"7kW vs 3.6kW home charger: which do you actually need?"** — Educational, positions InstallNest as the expert.

**Format:** Plain markdown pages on the site or as forum posts. No need for a blog infrastructure during validation.
